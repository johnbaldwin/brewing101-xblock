Once the grains have been crushed and put into a mash tun, water is added to begin the mashing process. This water is called the strike water. How much water, water temperature, how much grain, and grain temperature determine the mash temperature.

Strike water temperature Tw = (0.2 / R)(T2-T1) + T2
  • Tw - Strike water temperature in degrees Fahrenheit
  • R - Ratio of water to grain in quarts per pound
  • T1 - Initial temperature of the mash (grain before adding water)
  • T2 - Target temperature of the mash (grain after water is added)

If we have 10 quarts of water and 10 pounds of grain (for a ratio value of 1) and the grain is room temperature (70 °F), what temperature do we need for the strike water to get a target temperature of 155°F? Round your answer up to the next whole number.
